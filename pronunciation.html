📖 Finnish Pronunciation Guide

Finnish is one of the most phonetic languages in the world — what you see is what you say!

🔤 Vowels

Finnish has 8 vowels. Each has a clear, consistent sound:

Letter Sound English Example
a ah father
ä eh cat (British)
e eh bet
i ee macearooni
o oh more
ö uh iron (British)
u oo boot
y ew cue (round lips)

💡 Key Tip: The letters ä, ö, and y are unique to Finnish. Practice them!

ä = like the "a" in "cat" (but more open)

ö = round your lips for "o" but say "e"

y = round your lips for "u" but say "i"

🔊 Double Vowels

When a vowel is repeated, hold it longer:

aa = long "ah" — like "aah"
ää = long "eh" — like "eeh"
ii = long "ee" — like "eee"
öö = long "uh" — hold the sound

⚠️ Important: Double vowels are NOT diphthongs — they're the same sound held longer.

Hyvää = HY-vää (not "hy-vaa")

🔤 Consonants

Most consonants are similar to English:

Letter Sound English Example
b b boy
d d dog
f f fish
g g go
h h hot
j y yes
k k cat
l l love
m m man
n n no
p p pen
r rolled r perro (Spanish)
s s sun
t t top
v v very

💡 Special Note:

j = always sounds like English "y" (yes)

r = rolled/trilled (practice by saying "dd" quickly)

📏 Stress & Length

Stress Pattern

  • First syllable is always stressed: KY-llä, KI-itos, HY-vää
  • Secondary stress falls on every other syllable: TER-ve-TU-lo-a

Double Consonants

  • Double consonants are held longer: tt in hyvää is a crisp, held "t"
  • Teltta (tent) = TEL-tta (hold the "t")
  • Kissa (cat) = KIS-sa (hold the "s")

⚠️ Critical: Length changes meaning!

tuli = fire

tuuli = wind

tylli = (not a word, but illustrates the point)

🎯 Common Patterns

Vowel Harmony

Finnish words follow vowel harmony — front vowels (ä, ö, y) don't mix with back vowels (a, o, u) in the same word, except in compound words.

Common Letter Combinations

  • nk = "ng" as in sing → kenkä (shoe)
  • ng = "ng" as in long → kenkiä (shoes)
  • ht = "ht" as in light → huomenta
  • au = "ow" as in now → sauna
  • ai = "eye" → raitti
  • ei = "ay" → nei
  • oi = "oy" as in boy → oikea
  • ui = "wee" → kuusi (six)
  • yi = "yee" → pyyhi (wipe)
  • äy = "eye" → hyvä (good)

💡 Quick Tips

  • Read it as it's written — Finnish is phonetic!
  • Stress the first syllable — always!
  • Hold double letters — they change meaning
  • Round your lips for ö and y
  • Roll your r's — practice makes perfect
  • Speak clearly — each syllable gets equal time

🚴 For Cyclists: The pronunciation guide in the flashcards uses simplified English approximations. Listen to native speakers when possible, but these tips will get you started!
